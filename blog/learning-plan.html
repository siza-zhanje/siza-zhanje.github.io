What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term is to work as a Full Stack software developer. The dream for me would be developing software games even though I do not play computer games.

A description of your strengths and limitations when it comes to learning

I am very focused when I set myself to learn and easily remove anything that impedes my concentration levels. My best is finding out how to do things then attempting to apply what I have found, even though I get mixed results at times.

However, the perfectionist part of me means that I can take a long time to get a result. Working under extreme pressure makes me worry that the quality I want to present will be diminished in the rush to get everything done on time. The interesting effect is improved time management.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

During the course, I'd like to: learn better manage my stress levels increase positive self-thoughts and talk take time to check my understanding of what is required formulate my questions better so as to be easily understood Make myself available to my cohort members even when I am under pressure

I plan to keep up my daily exercise routine.

At the moment, my longest break is at the end of the day, when I've reignited the joy of cooking! During this time, I catch up with my family. Hearing their stories/needs takes my mind off.

I have been mostly, been following the advice to seek help when I haven't resolved any problems on my own within 15minutes. However, for the website challenge, that went out the window unfortunately. The trouble was started when I couldn't decide how to 'copy' the resource files onto my computer. So I went on to the nest step, following along on a random website. It seems that I didn't quite settle down as I “forgot” everything I'd learnt when I came to design & build my site! I worked into the night before I realised that I'd done what I shouldn't have done - go down a rabbit hole.

I requested a screen share with David which helped me immensely. His calmness was very re-assuring and I was able to complete the exercise. Even though he was busy, thank you for not sounding rushed.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

My commitment is to look through the Discord channels for one I believe best fits my question; to read through the posts to see if there may be an answer already. If not, post my question and wait up to 10 min for a reply. If no reply, to then reach out to David and/or Joseph, first; Gerard second; other facilitators; the Help Desk and finally after-hours-support.

I expect the facilitation team to keep up being timely in their responses to questions, being encouraging and patient. As David told me, to tell me know if I'm building something that will cause me issues does the track! I really appreciated him saying so. I expect protection from cohort/team members as was done during our Homegroup 'Catch-up' when David spoke to us about comparing ourselves to our past selves rather than comparing ourselves to other team members.
