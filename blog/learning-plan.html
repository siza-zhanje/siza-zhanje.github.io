Te Whare Tapa Whā - Well Being

Taha Tinana (physical wellbeing)

Every morning I get up early to exercise before I start work. At my desk is a flask of water which I sip often. I ensure that I get up & grab a fruit and/or refill my flask at every break. Getting a good night’s sleep especially during the week by switching off my computer by 9pm daily. Then spending at lest 30 min to unwind before bed.

Taha Hinengaro (emotional and mental wellbeing)

Remembering to practice positive self talk when I am struggling to find a solution even before reaching out of help. Thinking about how I phrase my questions so it is clear what I am asking increases my sense of achievement when my question is answered. Receiving regular hugs from my family helps me feel content and reciprocally, being kind when my children interrupt me!

Taha Wairua (spiritual wellbeing)

Before I start my day, I like to have a few minutes to wait on Adonai. He is my Identity and I am reminded of how I got to where I am.

Taha Whānau (family wellbeing)

Make time to catch up with my extended family in NZ & abroad - no matter how tired I get. My longest break is at the end of the day when I cook or help cook and enjoy sitting down with my family to dinner.

Whenua (land)

Walking on the grass and concrete ares of my garden barefoot does feel really nice on sunny days. I need to do it more often, even on cold days. The shock will wake me!

Boot Camp Learning Plan

Long term goal and/or career pathway

My long term goal is games development, possibly with back end development focus. However, I am leaning towards general applications development. Exposure to the back-end aspects of programming will help me make a decision. At the moment, I'd just like to gain more hands-on experience.

Biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp

Being very focused and motivated to learn; planning my day and spending time to reflect on that day's work and seeking help when I have exhausted my own efforts remain as my biggest strengths.

Being late to recognize when I am about to hit-the-wall is now my biggest limitation. This has happened twice during Foundations and both times, after long days with poor sleep quality, even though daily exercise and prayer, regular breaks, hydration, sunlight etc have been observed. This could be an issue when pair programming.

Biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp

Mental endurance to stay on course until the problem is solved will be a challenge for me. The first two JavaScript Carnival challenges took me longer to solve than the time box suggestions. I did not take a break in between so I found myself mentally drained going into the last of the challenges. This observation about me is new.

Human skills to developed while at Dev Academy

Clearly explaining a response when I offer to help a cohort member e.g in answering a question Discord; or when I am approached for help, to do so in a logical and simple way that makes their understanding easier.

As I write, I think when it comes to it, how I perform as a Driver during pair programming could be something I measure when I receive feedback. At present, I feel like my own thought process can be unordered despite my best pseudo code.

When I think of myself as a leader, I visualize myself leading from behind the scenes. Strange to think this as I do not consider myself an introvert! All the same, I would like to develop leadership skills especially as my self confidence in writing code grows.

My expectations from the Bootcamp team

Mainly to continue to be readily available, as they have been during Foundations. Their experience, compassion and patience have been stellar.

I believe the ratio will be better during boot camp so I would like more screen sharing opportunities when I get stuck. Posting my questions in Discord works for me when I know exactly what I am asking.

However, where it is a bit blurred with those obscure issues e.g. where my code should work but continues to fail with trouble shooting, David's well trained eyes have spotted the error almost immediately. This has got me going again a lot quicker.

Expectations of myself on Bootcamp

I expect myself to persevere no matter how hard I think it is, especially when I want to cry from exhaustion, frustration and missing my family. This, by reminding myself of the wonderful opportunity I have been blessed with and the fact that nine weeks is NOT a long time in the bigger scheme of things!

Above all I expect to back myself up by cultivating my not yet mind set and enjoying every clean concice working code that I write.

Just-in-Time learning has helped me keep up during Foundations, enabling me to hand in each sprint work well on time. My goal is to continue to apply this principle. Keeping up with the expected higher workload, I will be revisiting code written in earlier challenges more often than I have done during Foundations. Applying prior knowledge is what I also want to develop further during bootcamp.

To work productively, my mind needs to fully switch off by bed time and stay off until I start next day. Prayerful meditation is helping me in this area.
